You are the only person you can compare yourself to.


When we think of achievements, we tend to think about grades or degrees we got, amounts of miles we ran or weight we lost. However, what does or does not count as an achievement depends a lot on our situation. For a professional athlete, winning a trophy can be as much of an achievement as taking the first steps after a long injury. For a person with depression, being able to get out of bed or having the courage to ask somebody for help are huge achievements. Landing our dream job at 40 is as much of an achievement as landing our dream job at 18. Or yes, getting just any job after being unemployed for a long time

Any more examples? Sure!

  • I finally got married to the person I love.
  • Today, I finally filed for divorce.
  • I took time to see my family and spend time with my siblings
  • I stood up for myself when my parents tried to talk me down.
  • I apologized to somebody I hurt
  • I cut contact with somebody who keeps hurting me
  • I accepted an invitation and stepped out of my comfort zone
  • I declined an invitation and got some rest/me-time instead.
The achievements you list here are not necessarily about being productive and self-improve. Modern life pressures us enough on that. It can even be an achievement to realize that a previous achievement actually did not make you feel good and deciding to do it differently next time.
